Resonance

Privacy Policy

Effective: April 1, 2026  ·  Version 1.0

Resonance is a personal music journal. Your listening history, your ratings, your first impressions — these are yours. This policy explains plainly what we collect, why we collect it, and what we never do with it.

01  Who We Are

Resonance is an independent music journaling application. References to "Resonance," "we," "us," or "our" throughout this policy refer to the Resonance app and its developers. For questions about this policy, contact us at the address listed at the end of this document.

02  What We Collect

Account Information

When you create an account, we collect your email address and a password (stored as a secure hash — we never see your actual password). If you sign in with Google, we receive your name and email address from Google.

Profile Information

Information you choose to add to your profile: a display name or username, an optional profile photo, and an optional bio of up to 160 characters. All of this is voluntary — Resonance works without any of it.

Diary Entries and Ratings

The core of what Resonance stores: the albums you log, your scores, your written impressions, the timestamps of when you logged them, and whether an entry is a first listen or a relisten. This is your personal archive — it is the product.

Derived Taste Data

When you log an album, our backend automatically derives supplementary fields to power the Taste Map and Zodiac features: an emotional score, an accessible score, a genre cluster label, and a flag indicating whether the album falls within your Zodiac zone. These are computed from publicly available Spotify metadata (genre tags, album popularity, artist follower count) and your own rating behaviour — not from any personal data beyond your entries.

Social Activity

If you use Resonance's social features: who you follow, who follows you, and activity feed events generated by your actions (logging a first listen, changing your Mount Rushmore, receiving a Zodiac assignment). These are visible to your followers where applicable.

Usage Data

Standard technical information: device type, operating system version, app version, and crash reports. We use this to fix bugs and keep the app stable. We do not build advertising profiles from this data.

03  Spotify Integration

Connecting Spotify is entirely optional. Resonance is fully usable without it. If you choose to connect, here is exactly what we access and why.

What We Access

When you connect your Spotify account, Resonance requests permission to read the following data from Spotify's API:

  • Recently played tracks — to identify albums you've been listening to but haven't logged yet, and surface them as optional review prompts on your Home Screen.
  • Currently playing track — to populate the Currently Spinning slot on your shelf in real time, showing friends what you're listening to right now.
  • Top artists and top tracks (over 4-week, 6-month, and all-time windows) — to improve the accuracy of your Taste Zodiac axis calculations, particularly when you have fewer diary entries.

What We Do Not Access

We do not access your Spotify playlists, your saved library, your follower or following lists, your Spotify profile photo, or any financial or subscription information. We request only the minimum permissions required for the features described above.

How We Store It

Your Spotify access token and refresh token are stored securely on our servers and never exposed to the Resonance client application or to other users. We store a minimal mapping of recently played album IDs to identify unlogged listens — we do not store your complete Spotify listening history.

What We Never Do

We never automatically log albums from your Spotify history. Nothing appears in your diary without a deliberate action on your part — tapping "log it" in response to a prompt. We never sell, license, or share your Spotify data with third parties. We never use your Spotify data for advertising.

Disconnecting Spotify

You can disconnect Spotify at any time from your profile settings. When you disconnect, we delete your stored tokens and remove any pending Spotify prompt data. Your diary entries and Zodiac assignment are not affected — they are derived from your deliberate logs, not your Spotify history.

Spotify's Own Privacy Policy

When you connect Spotify, you are also subject to Spotify's Privacy Policy. We encourage you to read it.

04  How We Use Your Data

We use the data we collect to:

  • Operate and maintain your Resonance account and diary
  • Generate your Taste Zodiac archetype and Taste Map visualisation
  • Power the social features you choose to use — follows, activity feed, compatibility cards
  • Surface Spotify review prompts and Currently Spinning when you have connected Spotify
  • Diagnose and fix bugs and crashes
  • Improve Resonance based on how the app is used in aggregate

We do not use your data to serve advertisements. We do not build advertising profiles. We do not sell your data to any third party, ever.

05  What We Share

With Other Resonance Users

First listen entries are always public to your followers — this is a core feature of how Resonance works, and you are informed of it at the moment of logging. Your Taste Zodiac archetype is visible on your public profile. Your compatibility percentage with another user is visible to both of you on each other's profiles.

Your email address, your derived taste scores, your Spotify data, and any entries you have not made public are never visible to other users.

With Service Providers

We use the following third-party services to operate Resonance:

  • Firebase (Google) — database, authentication, and cloud functions. Your data is stored in Firebase Firestore.
  • Spotify — music metadata and, if you connect your account, listening data. Governed by Spotify's own terms.

We do not share your data with any other third parties.

Legal Requirements

We may disclose data if required to do so by law or in response to valid legal process. We will notify you if we receive such a request unless we are legally prohibited from doing so.

06  Your Data, Your Control

Export

You can export your complete diary, ratings, and shelf at any time from your profile settings in JSON or CSV format. No account deletion is required. Your data is always yours to take with you.

Deletion

You can delete your account from your profile settings. Deletion removes your account, all diary entries, your profile, and all associated data from our systems. Data deleted from Resonance is removed from Firebase within 30 days. Anonymised aggregate data (such as genre popularity statistics) that does not identify you may be retained.

Correction

You can edit or delete individual diary entries at any time, with the exception of sealed first listen entries which lock permanently by design — this is a feature you are explicitly informed about before submitting.

07  Children

Resonance is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

08  Security

We use industry-standard security practices: passwords are never stored in plain text, Spotify tokens are stored encrypted, and all data is transmitted over HTTPS. No system is perfectly secure — if you believe your account has been compromised, contact us immediately.

09  Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes to this privacy policy, we will notify you through the app or by email before the changes take effect. Continued use of Resonance after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy. The version date at the top of this document reflects when it was last updated.
